About

Structured thinking.
Relentless iteration.

My approach starts where most stop — at failure modes. I ask what breaks and why before I start building. That instinct came partly from getting things wrong: a pipeline that collapsed on edge-case input, a model that hit 95% in testing and fell apart in production. Every breakdown taught me something the success couldn't.

"The strongest work I've done has come out of teams that disagree well and debug together."

I've worked across a wide stack — GDB crash analysis, YOLOv8 inference, ETL automation, and now end-to-end document AI with custom knowledge graph extraction. Each domain has reinforced one lesson: architecture decisions compound. The 85% throughput gain in Scrapify wasn't from faster code — it was from rethinking the pipeline entirely.

As AI/ML Lead at the 10X Club, I've run workshops for peers and watched how explaining something clearly exposes every gap in your own understanding. Collaborative debugging sessions — where someone else sees the problem differently — have consistently led to better solutions than anything I'd have reached alone.
